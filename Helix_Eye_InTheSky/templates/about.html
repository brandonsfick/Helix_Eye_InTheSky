About Helix Eye In The Sky

Challenge

Many of today’s home security cameras send users an alert when any movement is detected and recorded. Movement can be caused by people, vehicles, wildlife, insects, or weather, such as moving tree limbs, blowing trash, rain, or snow – or even a change in ambient lighting. Receiving too many false positive alerts can be overwhelming, and can lead users to ignore alerts, or deactivate this important feature altogether. In other words, the system is crying wolf!

Solution

Helix Eye In The Sky is designed specifically to reduce the number of false positive alerts sent to a user. Helix Eye In The Sky does this by evaluating and identifying the content of a security camera’s images, rather than relying on just detected movement. Helix Eye In The Sky uses machine learning to filter out the false positive images, eliminating false alarms, sending alerts only when the presence of a person or unrecognized automobile is recorded by the camera.

The Future

Future work on Helix Eye In The Sky would focus on increasing the system’s ability to filter image content to reduce unnecessary false positive alerts.

  • Adding facial recognition capabilities would allow a user the ability to distinguish between “known” and “unknown” people.
  • Linking Helix Eye In The Sky with a user’s electronic calendar would allow the system to know when people or automobile movement is expected.
  • Implementing the ability for the user to “teach” the system would further refine image content identification and allow for a more customizable tool.
  • Incorporating the ability for a user using Helix Eye In The Sky on a mobile phone to call 911 with a button click to report suspicious activity captured by the camera.
